# Seasonal Garden Checklist

A consolidated task list for all plants in the garden.

---

## Before Spring (February - March)

### Pruning Window (Do Before Growth Starts)
- [ ] **Black Elder** — Main pruning: remove 1/3 oldest stems at ground level
- [ ] **Redcurrant** — Remove oldest stems, cut side shoots to 2-3 buds
- [ ] **Schersmin** — Do NOT prune now (only after flowering in July)

### Assessment & Preparation
- [ ] Check all plants for winter damage (don't prune yet except elder/redcurrant)
- [ ] Inspect **Rhododendron** for desiccation damage (brown leaf edges)
- [ ] Check **Boxwood** for winter bronzing (usually recovers)
- [ ] Check **Magnolia** for animal damage to bark
- [ ] Firm soil around **Magnolia** if frost heaving occurred

### Supplies to Order/Prepare
- [ ] Balanced fertilizer (for most plants)
- [ ] Acidifying fertilizer (for Rhododendron, Hydrangea)
- [ ] Frost protection fleece (for Magnolia blooms in April)
- [ ] Bird netting (for Redcurrant, Elder berries later)
- [ ] Mulch materials

---

## Spring (April - May)

### Fertilizing (April)
- [ ] **All plants** — Apply balanced slow-release fertilizer
- [ ] **Rhododendron** — Use acidifying fertilizer specifically
- [ ] **Hydrangea** — Use acid-loving plant formula

### Mulching (May)
- [ ] Apply 5-10 cm mulch around all plants
- [ ] **Rhododendron** — Use acidic mulch (pine needles, bark)
- [ ] Keep mulch away from stems/trunks

### Pruning (May - after flowering)
- [ ] **Magnolia** — Prune immediately after flowering (late May)
- [ ] **Rhododendron** — Deadhead spent flowers (critical!)

### Pest Watch
- [ ] Check **Boxwood** weekly for box tree moth caterpillars (May onwards)
- [ ] Watch for aphids on new growth (all plants)
- [ ] Inspect **Redcurrant** for sawfly larvae

### Other Tasks
- [ ] Water if spring is dry
- [ ] Trim **English Ivy** to control spread
- [ ] Remove suckers from **Elder** and **Magnolia**
- [ ] Protect **Magnolia** blooms from late frost (fleece)

---

## Summer (June - August)

### June — Peak Flowering & Harvest
- [ ] Enjoy **Schersmin** fragrance! (peak mid-June)
- [ ] **Harvest elderflowers** for cordial (sunny mid-June mornings)
- [ ] Install bird netting on **Redcurrant**
- [ ] Water deeply during dry spells (especially Hydrangea)

### July — Critical Pruning & Harvest
- [ ] **Schersmin** — Prune immediately after flowering! (Remove 1/3 oldest stems)
- [ ] **Harvest redcurrants** when fully red
- [ ] Trim **English Ivy** and **Boxwood** (second trim)
- [ ] Stop fertilizing all plants

### August — Final Tasks Before Autumn
- [ ] **Boxwood** — Final trim before mid-August
- [ ] **Harvest elderberries** when fully black (late Aug/early Sept)
- [ ] Stop all pruning (new growth won't harden before winter)
- [ ] Complete **Redcurrant** harvest

### Do NOT Prune After July
- Rhododendron, Hydrangea (macrophylla), Magnolia, Schersmin — flower buds forming!

---

## Before Winter (October - November)

### Critical: Water Before Freeze
- [ ] Deep water ALL plants before ground freezes
- [ ] Especially critical for: Rhododendron, Boxwood, Magnolia (shallow roots)

### Mulching for Winter
- [ ] **All plants** — Add 8-10 cm winter mulch around base
- [ ] **Rhododendron** — 10-15 cm of acidic mulch (pine needles)
- [ ] Keep mulch 10 cm away from trunks/stems

### Winter Protection (for vulnerable plants)
- [ ] **Hydrangea macrophylla** — Install chicken wire cage, fill with leaves, cover with burlap
- [ ] **Boxwood** — Install burlap windbreak on north/east sides
- [ ] **Magnolia** (young trees) — Wrap trunk with burlap
- [ ] **Rhododendron** (exposed) — Install windbreak screens

### No Protection Needed
- English Ivy (established), Schersmin, Black Elder, Redcurrant — fully hardy

### Cleanup
- [ ] Clean up fallen leaves around all plants
- [ ] Remove fallen berries from Elder (prevents unwanted seedlings)
- [ ] Check for coral spot on Redcurrant pruning wounds

### Don't Do
- ❌ Don't prune (except Elder can start in November)
- ❌ Don't fertilize
- ❌ Don't cut back Hydrangea stems (protects crown)

## Quick Reference: Pruning Calendar

| Plant | When to Prune | Never Prune |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| English Ivy | May, July | After August |
| Hydrangea (paniculata) | March-April | After July |
| Hydrangea (macrophylla) | After flowering only | Spring (removes buds) |
| Magnolia | Late May (after flowering) | July-March |
| Rhododendron | June (deadhead only) | After July |
| Schersmin | July (after flowering!) | Spring |
| Boxwood | May, July, early Aug | After mid-August |
| Black Elder | Feb-March | Autumn |
| Redcurrant | Feb-March | Autumn |

## Quick Reference: Harvest Calendar

| Plant | What | When | Notes |
|-------|------|------|-------|
| Black Elder | Flowers | Mid-June | Pick sunny mornings for cordial |
| Black Elder | Berries | Late Aug-Sept | Must be fully black; cook before eating |
| Redcurrant | Berries | July | Net against birds! |

## Monthly Reminders

### High-Priority Months

**March** — Last chance to prune Elder and Redcurrant before growth starts

**May** — Deadhead Rhododendron immediately after flowering

**July** — Prune Schersmin immediately after flowering (don't wait!)

**October** — Water deeply before ground freezes (critical for evergreens)
